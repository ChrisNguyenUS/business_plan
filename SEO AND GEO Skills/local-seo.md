# Local SEO Reference

Complete guide for ranking in Google's Local Pack (map + 3 listings) and optimizing
for local organic search. Read this file when working on any local service business.

---

## Local Pack vs Organic Search — Know the Difference

```
LOCAL PACK (map + 3 business cards)
  → Triggered by "near me", city-name, or location-intent queries
  → Ranking factors: GBP completeness, review count/recency, proximity to searcher
  → Appears ABOVE organic results — huge click share
  → Managed primarily through Google Business Profile

ORGANIC LOCAL RESULTS (10 blue links below the map)
  → Ranking factors: content quality, backlinks, technical SEO, local signals
  → Requires the full Topical Map + content strategy from SKILL.md
  → City-specific landing pages are the primary lever here

WIN BOTH: A complete GBP + strong location pages = Local Pack + organic placement.
```

## Google Business Profile (GBP) — Full Setup Guide

GBP is the single most important asset for local SEO. It controls your Local Pack ranking
and is the first thing most local searchers see.

### Step 1: Claim and Verify

```
1. Go to business.google.com
2. Search for your business name — if listed, claim it; if not, create it
3. Verify by:
   → Postcard (mailed to business address, 5–14 days)
   → Phone/email verification (instant, available for some businesses)
   → Video verification (Google may request a walkthrough video)
4. Do NOT skip verification — unverified profiles have limited ranking power
```

### Step 2: Complete Every Field

Completeness directly correlates with Local Pack ranking. Fill in everything:

```
BUSINESS NAME
  → Exact legal name only. No keyword stuffing ("Joe's Tax Prep - Best Houston Taxes")
  → Google will suspend profiles with keyword-stuffed names

CATEGORIES
  → Primary category: the most specific category that matches your core service
  → Secondary categories: all other services you offer
  → Example for tax preparer:
      Primary: "Tax Preparation Service"
      Secondary: "Notary Public", "Immigration Attorney" (if applicable), "Financial Consultant"

BUSINESS DESCRIPTION (750 characters)
  → First 250 characters show without clicking "More"
  → Lead with primary keyword + location + differentiator
  → Include services, languages spoken, credentials
  → Example: "Manna One Solution provides tax preparation, life insurance, and immigration
    document services in Houston, TX. Serving Vietnamese and Spanish-speaking clients
    since 2019. PTIN-registered tax preparer. Free initial consultation."

SERVICE AREA (for mobile/home-visit businesses)
  → List all cities and zip codes you serve
  → Do NOT list your home address if you don't serve clients there

HOURS
  → Accurate hours — Google penalizes businesses with incorrect hours (bad reviews hurt ranking)
  → Add special hours for holidays in advance

PHONE NUMBER
  → Use local area code, not 1-800 (local number = stronger local signal)
  → Must match exactly what's on your website (NAP consistency)

WEBSITE URL
  → Link to your homepage (or most relevant landing page)

ATTRIBUTES
  → Answer every applicable attribute: "Identifies as Latino-owned", "Vietnamese spoken",
    "By appointment only", "Online appointments", etc.
  → These appear in your profile and help GBP match your listing to relevant queries

PRODUCTS / SERVICES
  → Add every service with a name, description, and price (or price range)
  → These are indexed by Google and appear in your profile
```

### Step 3: Photos — Ongoing, Not One-Time

```
REQUIRED PHOTOS:
  ☐ Logo (square format, high resolution)
  ☐ Cover photo (1332 × 750px minimum)
  ☐ Interior photos (shows your workspace)
  ☐ Exterior photos (helps customers find you)
  ☐ Team photos (builds trust)

ONGOING CADENCE:
  → Add at least 1–2 new photos per week
  → Google's algorithm rewards active profiles
  → Photo types: team at work, client interactions (with permission), office, events, community

AVOID:
  → Stock photos (Google may flag them)
  → Photos with text overlays
  → Low-resolution or blurry images
```

### Step 4: GBP Posts

GBP Posts appear directly in your profile in search results. Treat them like mini-social posts.

```
POST TYPES:
  → Update: General news, announcements, seasonal messages
  → Offer: Limited-time promotions (include start/end date)
  → Event: Workshops, consultations, community events

CADENCE: At minimum 1 post per week
CONTENT: Short (150–300 words), includes a call to action, links to a relevant page
IMPACT: Active posting signals to Google that your business is engaged and current
```

## NAP Consistency — The Foundation of Local SEO

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. These must be **identical** everywhere online.

```
EXACT MATCH RULE:
  If your GBP says:   "Manna One Solution"
  Your website must:  "Manna One Solution" (not "Manna 1 Solution" or "Manna One Solutions")

  If your GBP says:   "Suite 400" 
  All directories:    "Suite 400" (not "Ste 400" or "#400")

  Phone format:       Pick one format and use it everywhere
                      ✅ (713) 555-0100  or  713-555-0100  or  +17135550100
                      ❌ Mix formats across platforms
```

### NAP Audit Process

```
1. Define your "canonical NAP" — the exact format you will use everywhere
2. Search Google for your business name to find all existing listings
3. Search "[business name] + city" to find directory listings
4. Correct any inconsistencies found (most directories allow free edits)
5. Use Moz Local (paid) or Whitespark (paid) to run automated NAP audits
```

## Citation Building

Citations = any online mention of your business's NAP. They function as trust signals
and contribute to Local Pack ranking even when they don't include a clickable link.

### Tier 1: Universal Citations (Every Business)

Submit to all of these regardless of business type:

```
Google Business Profile    → business.google.com (already covered)
Bing Places for Business   → bingplaces.com
Apple Business Connect    → business.apple.com (replaces Apple Maps Connect)
                            → Critical: Apple Intelligence + Siri use this data for AI answers
                            → Add: logo, photos, hours, services, action links (call, order, book)
                            → Prioritize alongside GBP — especially for iOS users
Yelp                       → biz.yelp.com/signup
Facebook Business Page     → facebook.com/business
Yellow Pages               → yp.com
Better Business Bureau     → bbb.org
Nextdoor Business          → nextdoor.com/business
```

### Tier 2: Industry-Specific Citations

```
TAX PREPARATION:
  → IRS Directory of Federal Tax Return Preparers (free, auto-listed when you have PTIN)
  → NATP (National Association of Tax Professionals) — if member
  → ProAdvisor directory (if QuickBooks certified)

INSURANCE:
  → State insurance department licensee search (auto-listed when licensed)
  → NAIC consumer lookup
  → NetQuote, EverQuote directories

GENERAL FINANCIAL SERVICES:
  → Thumbtack
  → Angi (formerly Angie's List)
  → HomeAdvisor
```

### Tier 3: Local Citations

```
→ Local Chamber of Commerce business directory
→ City/neighborhood business association
→ Local ethnic community organizations (e.g., Vietnamese Chamber of Commerce, Houston)
→ Local newspaper business directories
```

### Citation Building Rules

```
✅ Use exact NAP match on every submission
✅ Add a complete description (use your GBP description as a template)
✅ Add photos where the platform allows
✅ Include your website URL
✅ Submit manually for Tier 1 — use a service (BrightLocal, Whitespark) for Tier 2–3
❌ Never use automated spam submissions — Google can detect and discount these
```

## Review Strategy

Review count and recency are top-3 Local Pack ranking factors.
A business with 50 reviews at 4.8 stars outranks one with 5 reviews at 5.0 stars.

### How to Ask for Reviews (Ethical, Effective)

```
TIMING IS EVERYTHING:
  → Ask immediately after a positive interaction — while the experience is fresh
  → For service businesses: ask at the end of the appointment/service
  → Follow up within 24 hours via text or email if they didn't leave one in person

THE ASK:
  → Don't say "leave us a 5-star review" (review gating violates Google's terms)
  → Say: "If you had a good experience, a Google review helps other [Vietnamese/Spanish]
    families in Houston find our services. Here's the direct link: [short link]"

MAKE IT EASY:
  → Generate your Google review link: Google Maps → Your Business → Get more reviews
  → Shorten the link: g.page/yourbusiness or a URL shortener
  → Add the link to: email signatures, invoices, appointment confirmations, business cards

CHANNELS:
  → In-person: say it, show them the QR code
  → SMS: highest conversion rate (people read texts immediately)
  → Email: include in post-service follow-up email
  → WhatsApp/Zalo: for Vietnamese-speaking clients
```

### How to Respond to Reviews

```
POSITIVE REVIEWS (respond within 48 hours):
  → Thank them by first name
  → Mention the specific service (reinforces keyword signals)
  → Example: "Thank you [Name]! We're so glad the tax preparation process was smooth
    for your family. We'll be here every year — see you next filing season!"

NEGATIVE REVIEWS (respond within 24 hours):
  → Stay professional — your response is public and future customers read it
  → Acknowledge the issue, apologize, offer to resolve offline
  → Never argue or share private client details
  → Example: "Thank you for the feedback, [Name]. We're sorry the experience didn't
    meet your expectations. Please call us at [phone] so we can make it right."

FAKE/MALICIOUS REVIEWS:
  → Flag for removal via GBP dashboard (Report a review)
  → Document with screenshots before flagging
  → Don't respond in anger — respond professionally while the appeal is pending
```

## Local Content Strategy

Local Pack ranking depends on GBP. Organic local ranking depends on pages.

### Location Page Structure

For businesses serving multiple cities, create a dedicated page per location:

```
URL:     /tax-preparation-houston-tx/
         /tax-preparation-sugar-land-tx/

CONTENT MUST INCLUDE:
  → City name + primary service in H1
  → Local-specific details (neighborhoods served, local landmarks, driving directions)
  → Embedded Google Map
  → Local phone number or note about serving that area
  → LocalBusiness schema with areaServed set to that city
  → FAQ section with locally-relevant questions

AVOID:
  → Copy-paste pages — Google detects thin location pages and ignores them
  → Each page must have genuinely unique local content
```

### Neighborhood and Community Content

```
For businesses serving ethnic communities:
  → Content in the community's language (Vietnamese, Spanish) on dedicated URL paths
  → Community-specific FAQs: "Khai thuế ở Houston ở đâu?" (Where to file taxes in Houston?)
  → Mention community-specific services, cultural context, and trust signals
  → Partner content: mention local community organizations, Vietnamese/Hispanic chambers
```

## Local Pack Ranking — How Google Decides

```
PROXIMITY (you can't control this)
  → How close the searcher is to your business location
  → For service-area businesses, coverage overlaps — list all service cities in GBP

RELEVANCE (GBP optimization controls this)
  → How well your GBP categories, services, and description match the search query
  → The more complete and specific your GBP, the higher relevance score

PROMINENCE (reviews + citations + links control this)
  → Total number of reviews + recency + rating
  → Citation count and NAP consistency
  → Backlinks from local websites
  → Overall organic authority of your website

OPTIMIZATION PRIORITY:
  Week 1–2:  Verify GBP, complete all fields, add photos, submit Tier 1 citations
  Week 3–4:  Start review acquisition process
  Month 2:   Submit Tier 2–3 citations, start local content pages
  Month 3+:  Ongoing posts, photo additions, review responses, content updates
```
