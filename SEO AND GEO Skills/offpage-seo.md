# Off-Page SEO Reference

Guide for building backlinks and off-site authority. Read this file during Phase 9
(Off-Page SEO) of the main skill. On-page and technical SEO create the ceiling for
what you can rank for; backlinks determine whether you actually reach it.

---

## Why Backlinks Matter

Google's core insight: a link from one site to another is a vote of confidence.
Not all votes are equal — a link from a well-established, relevant site outweighs
hundreds of links from irrelevant or low-quality sites.

```
LINK VALUE FACTORS:
  Authority      → How well-established and trusted is the linking site?
  Relevance      → Is the linking site in the same or related niche?
  Placement      → In-content links (within article body) > sidebar/footer links
  Anchor text    → Descriptive anchor text passes more relevance signal
  Dofollow vs Nofollow → Dofollow passes ranking power; nofollow does not
                          (but nofollow links still drive traffic and brand awareness)
```

## Link Building Strategy by Business Type

### Local Service Business

Local businesses should prioritize local relevance over raw authority.

**Priority order:**

```
1. LOCAL CITATIONS (Phase 7 overlap)
   Every directory listing (Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages) is also a link.
   These are the easiest, fastest local authority signals.
   Complete Phase 7 (Local SEO) before any other link building.

2. CHAMBER OF COMMERCE
   → Join your local chamber and get listed in their business directory
   → Chamber links carry high local authority — Google trusts them
   → Cost: typically $200–500/year for membership
   → Also gets you: networking, referrals, and event listings

3. LOCAL SPONSORSHIPS
   → Sponsor local events, sports teams, school fundraisers, community organizations
   → Most sponsors get a link from the event/organization's website
   → Cost: varies ($100–2,000 depending on event scale)
   → Focus on: Vietnamese/Hispanic community organizations, local charities

4. LOCAL PRESS / NEWS COVERAGE
   → Pitch story angles to local news sites (Houston Chronicle, neighborhood blogs)
   → Story ideas: "Vietnamese-owned business helps community with tax season",
     "Local advisor explains new IRS changes for Houston families"
   → Press links are high-authority and often permanent

5. PARTNER / VENDOR LINKS
   → Any business you partner with, refer clients to, or receive referrals from
   → Ask to be listed on their website's "partners" or "resources" page
   → Example: real estate agents, mortgage brokers, immigration attorneys
```

### Blog / Content Site

```
1. GUEST POSTING
   → Write an article for another site in your niche; they link back to you
   → Find opportunities: "[your niche] + write for us", "[niche] + guest post"
   → Quality rule: only guest post on sites with real traffic and editorial standards
   → Avoid: paying for guest posts on link farms (Google penalizes this)

2. RESOURCE PAGE LINK BUILDING
   → Find pages that list resources in your niche ("best tax resources", "financial tools")
   → Search: "[niche] + resources", "[niche] + helpful links"
   → Pitch: "Hi, I noticed your resource page. I wrote [article] that your readers might
     find useful. Would you consider adding it?"

3. ORIGINAL RESEARCH / DATA
   → Publish original statistics, survey results, or data studies
   → Other sites in your niche naturally link to data they cite
   → Example: "Survey of 200 Houston Vietnamese families: tax filing challenges and solutions"

4. BROKEN LINK BUILDING
   → Find broken outbound links on relevant sites (use Check My Links Chrome extension)
   → Find a page on your site that could replace the broken link's content
   → Pitch the site owner: "Found a broken link on your page. My article covers the
     same topic and might be a good replacement."
```

### Any Business

```
UNLINKED BRAND MENTIONS
   → Find sites that mention your business name without linking to you
   → Search: "[Business Name]" -site:yourdomain.com
   → Email the site: "Thank you for mentioning us! Would you mind linking to
     [URL] so your readers can find us easily?"
   → Conversion rate is high — they already mentioned you, they like you

SUPPLIER / PARTNER LINKS
   → Any supplier, software company, or service provider you use
   → Many have customer/partner directories or "customers we serve" pages
   → Ask to be listed with a link

TESTIMONIALS AND CASE STUDIES
   → Offer to write a testimonial for a tool or service you use
   → Most companies publish these on their website with a link back to yours
```

## Competitor Backlink Analysis

Reverse-engineering competitor links reveals your fastest path to similar authority.

### Process

```
STEP 1: IDENTIFY COMPETITOR LINK SOURCES
  Tools:
    → Ahrefs Backlink Checker (free, limited to top 100 links)
    → Moz Link Explorer (free tier, 10 queries/month)
    → Google Search Console (only shows links TO YOUR site)

  Enter competitor URL → export their backlink list

STEP 2: CATEGORIZE LINK TYPES
  Group the links into categories:
    → Directories (easy to replicate)
    → Guest posts (medium effort to replicate)
    → Press/news (harder, requires outreach)
    → Partner/association links (varies)
    → Social profiles (instant, low-value)

STEP 3: IDENTIFY GAP OPPORTUNITIES
  → Which directories are they in that you're not? → Submit immediately
  → Which associations or groups do they belong to? → Join them
  → Which local sites link to multiple competitors but not you? → Priority outreach target

STEP 4: BUILD YOUR LINK TARGET LIST
  → Create a spreadsheet: URL, type, DR (domain rating), contact email, status
  → Prioritize by: relevance + authority + ease of acquisition
  → Work down the list systematically, tracking outreach and responses
```

## Outreach — How to Get Links Without Being Ignored

Most link-building outreach gets ignored. These principles improve response rates:

```
PERSONALIZATION
  → Reference something specific about their site or content
  → Never send a copy-paste template without personalization
  → Bad: "Hi, I love your website! Can we exchange links?"
  → Good: "Hi [Name], I read your article on [topic] and noticed you link to [X resource].
    I just published [article] which covers [specific angle] — your readers might find it
    more up-to-date. Would you be open to swapping the link?"

LEAD WITH VALUE
  → Always explain what's in it for them (their readers benefit, broken link fixed, etc.)
  → Your goal is secondary — their benefit is the pitch

FOLLOW UP ONCE
  → Wait 7 days, send one follow-up
  → Never send more than 2 emails to the same person

EMAIL FINDING TOOLS
  → Hunter.io (free tier: 25 searches/month)
  → RocketReach
  → LinkedIn (for business contacts)

RESPONSE RATE BENCHMARKS
  → Cold outreach: 5–15% response rate is normal
  → If below 5%, rewrite your pitch
  → Personalized + value-first: 15–25% response rate achievable
```

## Link Building Timeline

```
MONTHS 1–2:
  → Complete all local citations (Phase 7)
  → Join chamber of commerce
  → Set up partner link exchanges with 2–3 referral partners
  → Expected result: 10–20 quality local links

MONTHS 3–4:
  → Begin local press outreach (1–2 pitches/month)
  → Start guest posting outreach (find 5 target publications)
  → Identify and pursue sponsor opportunities
  → Expected result: 5–10 additional authority links

MONTHS 5–6+:
  → Guest posts should start publishing
  → Continue press outreach — consistency builds relationships
  → Begin unlinked brand mention campaign
  → Expected result: steady cadence of 2–5 new links/month
```

## Link Quality — How to Evaluate Before Pursuing

Not every link opportunity is worth pursuing. Evaluate each with these signals:

```
GOOD LINK INDICATORS:
  ✅ Site has real content (not just a directory or link farm)
  ✅ Site has real traffic (check Similarweb free estimate)
  ✅ Domain is relevant to your niche or location
  ✅ Link would be placed in the body of content, not a sidebar or footer
  ✅ Site has been indexed by Google (search: site:domain.com)

BAD LINK INDICATORS:
  ❌ Site exists only to sell links (usually offers links for $20–100 immediately)
  ❌ Site has no organic traffic
  ❌ Site has thousands of outbound links to unrelated sites
  ❌ Domain was recently registered (under 1 year old with no established content)
  ❌ Footer or sitewide links (same link appearing on every page)
```

## Toxic Links and Disavow

If your site has a history of bad links (from a previous SEO agency or spam), they can
actively harm your rankings.

```
SIGNS OF TOXIC LINK PROFILE:
  → Manual Action notification in Google Search Console
  → Rankings dropped suddenly after a Google update
  → Backlink audit shows many links from foreign spam sites, adult content, gambling

DISAVOW PROCESS:
  1. Run a full backlink audit (Ahrefs, Semrush, or Google Search Console)
  2. Identify toxic links — those from irrelevant spam sites with no real traffic
  3. Attempt to remove them: contact the site owner, ask for removal
  4. For links you cannot remove: add to disavow file
  5. Submit disavow file via Google Search Console → Disavow Links tool
  6. Format:
     # Disavowing spam links — [date]
     domain:spamsite1.com
     domain:spamsite2.com

  WARNING: Disavow file mistakes can harm good links. Use only when you have
  confirmed toxic links or a manual action. When in doubt, leave it out.
```

## Tracking Link Building Progress

```
METRICS TO TRACK MONTHLY:
  → Total referring domains (unique sites linking to you) — from Ahrefs/Moz/GSC
  → New links acquired this month
  → Lost links (monitor for link removals)
  → Domain Rating or Domain Authority trend (slow-moving, expect 3–6 months to see change)

WHAT TO RECORD IN YOUR OUTREACH SPREADSHEET:
  Columns: Target URL | Site name | DR | Type | Contact | Date contacted | Response | Status

REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS:
  → Month 1–2: Focus on easy wins (citations, partners). 10–20 links total.
  → Month 3–6: First authority links from press and guest posts. 30–50 total.
  → Month 6–12: Rankings start responding to link authority. 50–100+ referring domains.
  → "Overnight results" from link building don't exist. 6–12 months is normal.
```

## Social Media Signals

Google officially says social signals are not a direct ranking factor. However, Bing
explicitly uses social signals — and Bing's index powers ChatGPT browsing and Microsoft
Copilot. Strong social presence also drives branded search volume, which Google does use.

```
REQUIRED SOCIAL SETUP:
  ☐ Consistent brand profiles on: Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube (minimum)
  ☐ Instagram and TikTok if audience demographic warrants
  ☐ Profile info matches NAP exactly (name, address, phone)
  ☐ Website link on every profile

OPEN GRAPH META TAGS (every page):
  <meta property="og:title" content="Page Title">
  <meta property="og:description" content="Page meta description">
  <meta property="og:image" content="https://yoursite.com/images/og-image.jpg">
  <meta property="og:url" content="https://yoursite.com/page-url/">
  <meta property="og:type" content="website">

TWITTER CARD META TAGS (every page):
  <meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">
  <meta name="twitter:title" content="Page Title">
  <meta name="twitter:description" content="Page meta description">
  <meta name="twitter:image" content="https://yoursite.com/images/og-image.jpg">

SOCIAL ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY:
  → Share every new page/blog post on all social profiles
  → Engage in Vietnamese/Hispanic community Facebook Groups (see community-seo.md)
  → Post GBP updates weekly (covered in local-seo.md)
  → Social engagement creates "fresh signals" that complement content freshness

WHY BING SOCIAL SIGNALS MATTER:
  → Bing considers Facebook/LinkedIn shares as relevance signals
  → Content shared widely on social may rank better on Bing
  → Bing → ChatGPT browsing + Copilot = social presence affects AI citations
```
